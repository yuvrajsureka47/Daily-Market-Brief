Kevin Warsh Takes Fed Chair on Jerome Powell's Last Day
The Senate confirmed Kevin Warsh 54-45 as new Federal Reserve Chair on Wednesday — and today, May 15, marks Powell's last day in office. Warsh inherits a brutal mandate: US CPI hit 3.8% in April (highest since May 2023), PPI surged 6% year-on-year, and markets are now pricing a 28-30% probability of a rate hike by December. Warsh's past hawkish instincts clash with Trump's push for lower rates. The critical risk is that any dovish move by Warsh gets read as political capitulation, which could destabilize the Fed's credibility and push yields sharply higher.

India Raises Gold Import Tariff to 15% — Demand Signal and Inflation Control
India raised import tariffs on gold and silver from 6% to 15%, a significant policy move that directly compresses India's gold demand — the world's second largest. The timing is deliberate: gold near $4,680/oz has driven high import bills and pressured the current account deficit. The RBI holds its repo rate at 5.25% with a neutral stance, projecting FY27 GDP at 6.9%. The tariff hike is a fiscal tool to manage trade balance pressure without tightening monetary policy further.
Elevated Oil Prices Threaten India's Macro Balance
With Brent crude above $105 per barrel, India — which imports over 80% of its oil — faces mounting imported inflation. The IEA warns global oil markets will remain undersupplied until October even if the Strait of Hormuz reopens next month. For India, this translates directly into higher fuel subsidies, a wider current account deficit, and pressure on the rupee. Goldman Sachs forecasts India's current account deficit widening to $37 billion in 2026. The RBI's inability to cut rates aggressively in this environment is also squeezing credit growth.
