RBI's June 5 Capital Flow Package to Unlock $40 to $50 Billion in Foreign Inflows
RBI's June 5 monetary policy meeting delivered a major capital flow package designed to stabilise the rupee without raising rates. Key measures include: fully subsidising FX hedging costs for banks raising 3 to 5 year FCNR(B) deposits; a concessional FX swap facility for state-owned enterprise external commercial borrowings; and the government retrospectively removing capital gains and interest withholding taxes on government securities for foreign investors from April 1, 2026. The government also expanded the list of G-Secs available under the Fully Accessible Route. MUFG estimates these steps could generate approximately $40 billion in inflows, with potential for over $50 billion if India gets included in the Bloomberg Global Agg Index. The 10-year G-Sec yield is expected to trade around 7%, which combined with zero withholding tax, makes Indian bonds increasingly attractive to global fixed income investors.

Great American AI Act — Congress Proposes 3-Year Freeze on State AI Laws
Representatives Obernolte and Trahan released a 269-page draft federal AI framework on June 4 — the Great American Artificial Intelligence Act. Its headline provision is a three-year preemption of all state AI laws related to frontier model development. This would freeze California's AI bills, Colorado's AI Act (set for June 30 implementation), and all other state-level AI regulations at the federal level for three years if passed. Companies with over $500 million in annual gross revenue must publish Frontier AI Frameworks and report critical safety incidents to the government. This is the first serious attempt at unified federal AI governance in the US and creates regulatory clarity for large labs. It may ease compliance burdens for OpenAI and Anthropic ahead of their IPOs, but also signals that Congress now views frontier AI regulation as a federal prerogative.
