Regulation / AI Enforcement
Colorado AI Act Enforcement Begins Tomorrow (June 30) — First AI Liability Law on US Soil
The Colorado Consumer Protections for Artificial Intelligence Act takes effect June 30, 2026 — tomorrow. It requires organisations deploying high-risk AI systems (employment, healthcare, finance, education, housing, legal services) to implement risk management programs, conduct annual impact assessments, issue disclosure when AI drives consequential decisions, and allow affected individuals to appeal. Enterprise AI deployments across most large companies are already in scope. The federal Great American Artificial Intelligence Act proposed a 3-year preemption of state laws — but that bill has not passed, so Colorado's deadline stands. This is the first hard AI enforcement deadline in US history and a landmark shift from voluntary best-practice frameworks to legal liability. Companies that haven't already prepared are now exposed.
