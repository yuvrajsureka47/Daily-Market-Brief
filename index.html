Global Macro
United States
US–Iran Peace Talks Stall on Uranium and Strait Control
Iran's Supreme Leader has directed that near-weapons-grade uranium must not leave the country — directly contradicting a core US demand. Tehran has also announced a "Persian Gulf Strait Authority" to enforce controlled maritime passage through Hormuz. Trump says he will wait "a few days" before resuming pressure. This is the dominant risk variable across oil, equities, and bonds. Until a deal is confirmed, $95–$105 WTI is the operating range markets are pricing in.
Fed Minutes: December Rate Hike Back on the Table
The latest FOMC minutes show most policymakers believe an additional rate increase remains warranted if inflation stays above 2%. Markets currently price about a 40–50% chance of a 25bps hike in December. The 10Y yield has been oscillating near 4.6%, creating a ceiling on equity valuations. Higher-for-longer is now the base case through at least mid-2027 for the first cut.
Government Awards $2 Billion to Quantum Computing Firms
The Trump administration is deploying CHIPS Act capital into quantum computing: IBM gets $1 billion, GlobalFoundries $375 million, and D-Wave, Rigetti, and IonQ each receive roughly $100 million, with government equity stakes attached. Rigetti jumped over 30%, D-Wave surged 22%. This signals a structural shift — the US government now views quantum computing as a strategic national asset, not just a research curiosity.
India
Sensex Opens Higher on Peace Deal Optimism; Tech Stocks Weigh
The Sensex opened around 75,500 on Friday, recovering from Thursday's 135-point drop, with HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, and Bajaj Finserv as top gainers. Technology stocks remain the weak spot — Infosys and Tech Mahindra fell over 1.3% amid FII outflows and global demand concerns. India VIX had eased to 17.60, suggesting reduced near-term panic, but the market remains in a broad sideways range. India's HSBC PMI flash readings for May showed private sector expansion moderated, reflecting softer global demand and Middle East pressure.
Rupee Under Structural Pressure Near Record Lows
The Indian rupee recently hit record lows near Rs 96.82 against the dollar before recovering slightly to Rs 96.30. A combination of elevated oil import costs, FII selling in equities, and a broadly stronger dollar in a higher-for-longer rate environment is structuring the pressure. For Indian markets, a weak rupee compounds the inflation problem and narrows RBI's room to cut rates.
Europe
ECB Holds Rates Amid Energy Shock; GDP Contractions Forecast Across Eurozone
The ECB is keeping policy rates on hold while monitoring how the Iran conflict passes through to energy prices and core inflation. S&P Global now forecasts short-lived GDP contractions in Germany, France, Italy, and the UK in 2026. An additional round of ECB rate hikes has been priced in by S&P staff. The "second China shock" — Chinese exports flooding euro area markets as the US cut low-value Chinese imports — is adding disinflation in goods but creating a structural competitiveness threat for European industry.
China & APAC
Chinese AI Models Now Command 60% of Developer Usage on OpenRouter
Databricks CEO disclosed that Chinese AI models — DeepSeek V3.2, Kimi K2.6, and Zhipu GLM-5.1 — grew from 1% of usage on OpenRouter in 2024 to over 60% by May 2026. These models are now benchmark-competitive with OpenAI and Anthropic at a fraction of the cost. This is not just a tech story — it is a market share and geopolitical signal about where AI infrastructure spending is migrating globally.
Japan Q1 GDP Beats Estimates at 2.1% Annualized
Japan grew at an annualized 2.1% in Q1 2026, well above the 1.7% consensus estimate and ahead of the prior quarter's 1.3%. This positions Japan as one of the stronger performing developed economies this year. However, the improvement predates the full impact of the Iran-driven energy shock on Asian manufacturing chains, so Q2 will be the real test.

IPO Filing
OpenAI Files Confidential S-1 With SEC — September Debut in View
OpenAI is filing a confidential IPO prospectus today with Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley as bookrunners, targeting a September 2026 listing at a valuation of $852 billion to $1 trillion. This follows a record $122 billion funding round and a legal win over Elon Musk that removed a major structural obstacle. If it lists this year, it would be one of the largest public market debuts in history. Anthropic and SpaceX are also targeting 2026 listings. The AI sector is entering a new phase — from private capital formation to public market accountability.

Anthropic Projects $10.9 Billion in Q2 Revenue — Up 130% QoQ

Alongside a reported $900 billion valuation and October 2026 IPO target, Anthropic is emerging as the primary rival to OpenAI for institutional capital allocation in the AI sector.

Key Takeaways
1
The US–Iran conflict is now the single most important macro variable for global markets. WTI above $95 sustains inflation above the Fed's 2% target, which keeps December rate hike risk alive and suppresses equity multiple expansion. Until the Strait of Hormuz reopens fully and credibly, this risk does not go away — and Iran's latest uranium directive suggests a deal is not imminent.
2
Bond markets are sending a warning that equity markets are choosing to partially ignore. The 30Y briefly touched 5.19% this week — a near-19-year high. The 10Y at 4.6% is not yet breaking the bull market, but CNBC's own analysis flagged the specific yield level that could. Watch for any move above 4.75% on the 10Y as a stress trigger for rate-sensitive sectors.
3
The AI IPO wave is structurally significant for capital markets. OpenAI at $850B–$1T, Anthropic at $900B, and SpaceX at $1.75T entering public markets in the same six-month window would absorb substantial institutional and retail capital — redirecting flows from other sectors and creating benchmark weight implications once listed.
4
India's market is in a holding pattern with asymmetric risk. On the upside: a US–Iran deal would cut oil prices sharply, ease rupee pressure, and trigger a relief rally. On the downside: sustained elevated oil, continued FII selling in IT and large-caps, and a rupee at 96 plus adds imported inflation and limits RBI's policy flexibility. Domestic banks and autos are the relative safe zones.
5
The next 72 hours are high-stakes. Watch: (a) whether OpenAI's S-1 actually arrives at the SEC today, which would be a landmark event; (b) any signal from Trump or Iran's new leadership on uranium storage — this alone can move oil by $5–$10 in a session; and (c) Nvidia's earnings interpretation as they feed into the AI spend narrative that underpins the entire tech rally.
