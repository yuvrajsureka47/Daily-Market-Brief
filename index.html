ADP Payrolls Miss: 98,000 Jobs Added in June vs. 110,000 Expected
Private sector hiring decelerated sharply in June, with ADP clocking 98,000 new jobs against a Dow Jones consensus of 110,000. Nearly half the gains came from education and health services alone (48,000), while leisure and hospitality added just 2,000 for a sixth straight weak month. This sets up Thursday's official NFP report (BLS) as the key event of the week, with Wall Street expecting 115,000 jobs and unemployment steady at 4.3%. A weak print would raise recession risks; a strong one puts the Fed back in hiking territory. The market cannot afford ambiguity here.
Fed Chair Warsh Reaffirms 2% Inflation Target at ECB Forum, Offers No Rate Guidance
Speaking at the ECB Forum in Portugal, new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh stated that "prices are too high" but provided no forward guidance on policy — a deliberate departure from the Powell-era communication playbook. This opacity is itself the message: markets can no longer anchor on Fed dot plots the way they used to. Nine of 18 FOMC officials expect at least one rate hike in 2026, with the Fed funds rate currently parked at 3.50%–3.75%. Any NFP upside tomorrow would sharply reprice rate expectations higher, compressing equity multiples.

Eurozone Flash PMI Improves to 49.5 in June, Though Germany Remains in Contraction

AI Watch
Infrastructure Strategy
Meta Announces Cloud Business to Monetize $145B AI Capex — Enters Direct Competition with AWS, Azure, GCP
Bloomberg reported on July 1 that Meta is building a cloud infrastructure business to sell excess AI compute capacity, following SpaceX's xAI playbook. Meta's stock surged 9–10% on the day. The announcement has far-reaching implications: CoreWeave fell 13% and Nebius fell 14% as the market priced in competitive pressure on neocloud providers. SpaceX itself had signed deals with Anthropic ($1.25 billion/month) and Google ($920 million/month) for compute access. Meta's entry signals that hyperscalers with massive self-built AI infrastructure are increasingly competing with the cloud market rather than buying from it. This shifts the AI infrastructure investment thesis away from third-party compute providers and toward companies with captive demand and proprietary scale.
Regulation
White House Issues Executive Order on AI Security: Voluntary Pre-Release Access for Federal Vetting of Frontier Models
The White House issued an executive order on June 2, 2026 establishing a voluntary framework allowing AI developers to share frontier models with the federal government up to 30 days before release for national security vetting. The order also mandates an AI cybersecurity clearinghouse within 30 days, coordinating vulnerability scanning across critical infrastructure. Critically, the order does not create mandatory licensing or preclearance requirements, preserving the administration's pro-innovation stance. For AI companies, this creates soft pressure to engage government review without regulatory hard stops. The framing is cooperative rather than punitive, and the market is pricing it as AI-positive.
