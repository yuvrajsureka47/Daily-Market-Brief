🇨🇳 China
China's June Manufacturing PMI Beats at 50.3 — AI Exports Drive the Gain
China's official NBS Manufacturing PMI rose to 50.3 in June from 50.0 in May, beating the 50.1 consensus. The headline number masks a pronounced bifurcation: high-tech manufacturing PMI surged to 53.5, fuelled by global AI infrastructure demand, while consumer goods and real estate-adjacent production remained sluggish at 50.2. New orders returned to expansion (51.2 from 49.9), and export orders turned positive (50.1 from 48.6) — both driven by AI hardware shipments. The structural risk is clear: China's recovery is entirely export-driven and concentrated in a handful of tech supply chain sectors. Any slowdown in hyperscaler capex globally would hit Chinese factories disproportionately.
PBOC in Wait-and-See Mode; No Rate Cuts Expected in 2026
Despite uneven domestic momentum, China's central bank is holding off on additional easing, with BBVA Research maintaining its 2026 GDP forecast at 4.5% and ruling out rate or reserve ratio cuts for the rest of the year. Geopolitical risk easing from the US-Iran truce is a modest tailwind, as it reduces energy import costs for China, one of the world's largest crude oil importers. However, domestic demand, property market weakness, and employment sub-indices continuing to contract (48.4 in June) underscore that the recovery remains hollow at its core.
