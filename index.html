Key Takeaways
1
The entire global market narrative for June pivots on one binary: whether the US-Iran ceasefire extension is formalized and the Strait of Hormuz reopens. A successful deal is worth approximately $10 to $15 per barrel off oil prices, which directly lowers PCE inflation, potentially unlocking Fed rate cuts in Q1 2027. A deal failure sends oil back above $100, keeps the Fed locked, compresses equity multiples, and sends India's trade deficit and rupee into a negative feedback loop. Watch for Trump's formal sign-off in the next 48 to 72 hours.

India is the most exposed major emerging market to an Iran deal failure. The rupee at 95.27, FII outflows of Rs 21,106 crore in a single session, a weak monsoon forecast, and oil-driven import cost pressure all compound simultaneously. DIIs are absorbing the FII selling for now, but their capacity is finite. If the Nifty breaks 23,500 decisively, institutional risk-off could accelerate sharply into June. The MSCI rebalancing provides some support, but net the picture is structurally fragile.
3
Anthropic's $65 billion raise at a $965 billion valuation and $47 billion revenue run-rate is not just an AI company event — it is a macro event. It validates the AI infrastructure spending cycle and confirms that semiconductor suppliers (SK Hynix, Micron, TSMC) have sustained demand regardless of the geopolitical backdrop. The Anthropic and OpenAI dual IPO story is the largest structured capital markets event building for late 2026, likely to draw significant institutional allocation away from traditional asset classes into AI-adjacent equity.
4
China's PMI at exactly 50.0 — the borderline between growth and contraction — masks a concerning internal divergence: production is expanding (51.2) but new domestic orders are contracting (49.9). This is a demand-deficit problem, not a capacity problem. Beijing's 15th Five-Year Plan is pushing high-tech manufacturing into growth, but without domestic demand recovery, China's economic outperformance remains export-dependent and thus highly sensitive to US-China trade dynamics and global shipping normalization.
5
The BOJ's June 16-17 meeting is the second-biggest macro event in APAC after the Hormuz resolution. A rate hike would trigger the most significant yen appreciation move in years, unwinding the massive carry trade (borrow in yen, invest in higher-yielding assets). This carry unwind has historically caused sharp, cross-asset dislocations — particularly for emerging market currencies and risk assets. Indian equities, already under FII pressure, would face additional headwinds if global liquidity conditions tighten through a BOJ policy normalization.
