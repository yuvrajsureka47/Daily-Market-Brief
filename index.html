India
Nifty Holds Near 23,720 — FII Selling Offsets DII Support
The Nifty 50 closed at 23,719 on Friday, up 0.27%. GIFT Nifty signals a green open today at approximately 23,765 (+0.19%). Nifty Bank was the standout, rising 1.15%. FIIs sold Rs 4,440 crore on May 22, while DIIs absorbed Rs 6,003 crore — keeping the market from falling. India is in a structural tug of war: strong domestic institutional buyers vs. continued foreign portfolio outflows driven by the global risk premium from the Iran conflict and elevated US yields.
RBI May Bulletin: Economy Resilient, But Import Bill Swells
India's May 2026 RBI Bulletin confirms domestic economic resilience in April despite West Asia uncertainty. CPI inflation edged up to 3.5%, driven by food prices. The merchandise trade deficit widened meaningfully — crude oil and gold imports are the primary culprits. The government raised customs duties on gold, silver, and platinum in response. The RBI repo rate remains at 5.25%, projecting GDP growth of 6.9% for FY27. Monsoon season will be a key determinant of the next few months.
Europe
Geopolitical Risk
UK PMI Slips to 48.5 — Contraction Territory for First Time in 13 Months
Flash UK PMI for May dropped to 48.5, the first sub-50 reading in over a year. PMI below 50 means economic activity is shrinking — in this case driven by services sector weakness. The Middle East war's energy shock is visibly flowing through into European demand destruction. The ECB is simultaneously dealing with rate hike pressure from inflation and growth contraction risk — a stagflation-adjacent scenario that limits its room to maneuver.
ECB Holds Rates, Revises Growth Forecasts Downward
S&P Global and the ECB have both lowered growth forecasts for 2026 across major European economies. The ECB's base case now includes additional rate hikes in Western Europe this year. Germany, France, and Italy are all at risk of quarter-on-quarter GDP contractions. European sovereign bond yields are under upward pressure, with UK gilts particularly affected by political uncertainty and fiscal cost concerns from the prolonged energy shock.
China
Manufacturing PMI Holds at 50.3 — Expansion Continues But Pace Slows
China's official NBS manufacturing PMI printed at 50.3 in April, the second consecutive month of expansion — but the pace is visibly slowing. Energy-intensive sectors are being hit by the oil shock, consumption remains weak, and property markets continue to deteriorate. May's PMI reading this week will be critical. A print below 50 would signal contraction and add to global growth concerns. The IEA already flagged a Q2 2026 global oil demand contraction of roughly 1.5 million barrels per day — concentrated heavily in the Middle East and Asia-Pacific.
Geopolitical Risk
PLA Naval Activity Intensifies Near Japan and Philippines
China's PLA Eastern Theater Command sent task groups through two sensitive Japanese waterways in late April, following Japan's first combat role participation in the Balikatan exercises with the US and Philippines. The PLA Southern Theater Command deployed at least 14 ships — including the Liaoning carrier — for live-fire drills east of Luzon. This is a structural escalation in Indo-Pacific posturing and adds a second geopolitical risk axis to markets already preoccupied with the Middle East.
APAC
Australia Hikes Rates Third Time in 2026 — RBA Now at 4.35%
The Reserve Bank of Australia raised its policy rate by 25 basis points to 4.35% in an 8-1 vote — the third consecutive hike of 2026. RBA staff project headline CPI to peak at 4.8% in Q2, with core inflation remaining above target through end-2027. Australia's aggressive tightening cycle is a preview of what other central banks may be forced into if oil-driven inflation persists. It also signals real stress in Asia-Pacific demand, where energy import costs are most acute.

Key Takeaways
1
The Iran conflict is the fulcrum of every major market variable right now. Oil price drives inflation expectations. Inflation expectations drive Fed policy bets. Fed bets drive the 10Y yield. The 10Y yield determines the cost of capital for all risk assets — equities, crypto, and emerging markets alike. A confirmed peace deal would be the single largest positive macro catalyst of 2026. Watch the Strait of Hormuz reopening as the clearest signal, not press conferences.
2
India is the clearest structural beneficiary in this environment, but the current account is a live risk. The RBI Bulletin confirms domestic resilience but also flags a widening trade deficit driven by oil and gold imports. With FIIs still net sellers and DIIs compensating, Nifty is holding up on domestic faith rather than foreign conviction. A prolonged oil shock tightening India's import bill could force a policy response that markets are not pricing.
3
The AI IPO wave (SpaceX, OpenAI, Anthropic) represents the largest capital reallocation event coming to public markets since 2004-2012. These listings will absorb significant institutional liquidity and will compete with existing tech holdings for portfolio weight. The near-term effect could be rotation out of earlier-stage tech names and into these high-conviction new listings. Watch how Nvidia's stock responds post-IPO filings — any weakness there is a leading indicator of institutional rebalancing.
4
Bitcoin's inability to close above its 200-day moving average despite a regulatory tailwind (CLARITY Act) and peace deal optimism tells you where institutional priority currently sits. Capital is still preferring 4.5% Treasury yields over crypto risk. The unlocking event for BTC would be a confirmed Fed pivot signal or peace deal reducing the rate hike probability materially. Until then, BTC is likely to remain rangebound in the $70K–$82K zone.
5
Watch this week: China's May NBS PMI print (a sub-50 reading would be bearish globally), US Q1 GDP data and initial jobless claims on May 28, and any Strait of Hormuz shipping data. Europe is already in contractionary PMI territory — if China joins, the global demand picture deteriorates rapidly and central banks face a genuine stagflation dilemma with no clean solution.
