Hardware Consolidation
Qualcomm in Talks to Buy Tenstorrent for $8-10B — Race to Dethrone Nvidia Intensifies
Qualcomm is reportedly in advanced talks to acquire Tenstorrent for between $8B and $10B, a sharp jump from the $3.2B valuation the startup targeted in its last funding round. Tenstorrent, led by legendary chip architect Jim Keller (formerly Apple, AMD, Tesla), builds AI processors on open RISC-V architecture and claims efficiency advantages over Nvidia GPUs for specific workloads. The deal fits Qualcomm's pivot away from its smartphone business, following its $2.4B acquisition of Alphawave Semi. QCOM shares jumped over 4% on the news, and rose another 14% after Qualcomm Investor Day where management raised non-handset revenue targets significantly. Strategic importance: Nvidia's near-monopoly in AI training chips is attracting sustained M&A pressure. If Qualcomm, Intel, and AMD all build viable alternatives via acquisitions, it could compress Nvidia's pricing power and margins over a 3-5 year horizon — but that window is still far off.
