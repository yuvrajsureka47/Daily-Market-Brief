Micron Crosses $1 Trillion Market Cap — HBM Memory Shortage Reshapes Chip Hierarchy
Micron Technology crossed the $1 trillion market cap mark for the first time on May 26, with its stock more than tripling in 2026 alone. Key Takeaways
1

The Strait of Hormuz is the single most important variable in global markets right now. Every piece of conflicting information from Washington and Tehran moves oil 3–5% in a session. Until a credible, verified deal is signed and shipping fully resumes, this whipsaw pattern continues — and equities, oil, gold, and currencies will remain hostage to every headline out of Doha.

2

The AI chip hierarchy is being rewritten in real time. Nvidia's GPU monopoly for training is now being flanked by an ARM CPU boom for agentic AI inference workloads. The Snowflake-AWS Graviton deal and Meta's Graviton commitment together signal that the next phase of enterprise AI infrastructure spending will split between Nvidia (training) and ARM/AWS (orchestration). Memory companies — Micron, SK Hynix, Samsung — are the new monopolies; every AI cluster needs their HBM chips and there is none to spare through 2026.

Gold falling 15% since the conflict started is a counterintuitive but important signal: markets are betting on a deal, not on a prolonged supply shock. That would be the biggest macro regime shift of 2026.
