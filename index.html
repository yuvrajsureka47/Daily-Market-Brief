MARKET STRUCTURE
GitHub Copilot's shift to usage based billing triggers developer backlash on its first full billing cycle
Developers are reporting projected monthly costs jumping from $29 to $750, and from $50 to $3,000 for heavy agentic workflows, after Copilot moved off flat rate subscriptions on June 1. GitHub's CPO confirmed a single long running agentic session can cost as much as a month of basic chat usage. Strategic importance: this is the clearest public signal yet that flat rate AI coding subscriptions are economically unsustainable at scale. Cursor and Windsurf made similar moves earlier in the year, and the market looks to be settling on a roughly 20 dollar light tier and a roughly 200 dollar power tier structure across the industry.
