Infrastructure Deal
Reflection AI Signs $150M/Month Deal for Nvidia GB300 Chips via SpaceX Colossus 2
AI startup Reflection AI committed to paying $150 million per month beginning July 1, 2026 through 2029 for access to Nvidia's latest GB300 AI chips at SpaceX's Colossus 2 data center. This is one of the largest per-month infrastructure commitments in AI history. It signals that frontier AI labs are locking in compute access years in advance, reflecting genuine supply scarcity in high-end AI hardware. For Nvidia, this is direct evidence the GB300 upgrade cycle is commercially real. For SpaceX, Colossus 2 is now a serious enterprise infrastructure business.

Geopolitics / AI Export War
Beijing Blacklists 56 US Firms After Washington Pulls Anthropic Models; Export War Turns Bilateral
Ten days after the US restricted Anthropic's top models from foreign access, China retaliated by blacklisting 56 American firms. The move marks a structural escalation: what began as US unilateral AI export controls has now become a two-sided technology denial campaign. This signals that AI model access is now a geopolitical asset class — similar to semiconductors — with significant implications for how global AI value chains are structured over the next decade.

This is good for consumers and should reduce headline PCE. However, the Fed's hawkish pivot is no longer driven by energy alone — core services and shelter inflation remain stubborn. Traders should not mistake oil relief as a catalyst for rate cut optimism. The Thursday PCE print (May, YoY consensus 4.1%) will be the real test.
4
Gold falling while rate hike expectations rise is textbook monetary policy sensitivity — gold generates no yield, so it loses attractiveness versus Treasuries when real yields move up. The fact that gold is down even as geopolitical risk persists tells you the market is pricing in higher rates more aggressively than safe-haven demand. Watch gold closely around the Thursday PCE release: a hotter-than-expected print would pressure it further toward $4,000.
5
The AI export war is now bilateral — China has moved from passive recipient of US AI restrictions to active retaliator. This creates a bifurcating global AI market with enormous implications for Indian IT companies (opportunity as neutral ground), for European AI policy (forced to pick sides), and for AI hardware (Nvidia's addressable market is now structurally smaller than its revenue charts suggest). The 24-72 hour window to watch: China's response to the US-Iran diplomatic progress, which reduces Washington's external pressure and frees it to double down on tech export controls.
