Oil is now the dominant macro variable globally. Every other data point — CPI, PPI, Fed expectations, rupee pressure, ECB policy, corporate margins — flows from the Hormuz shutdown. Until there is a credible resolution, the base case for markets should treat elevated energy as structural, not transient. Ten of the last 12 recessions were preceded by an oil spike. The clock is running.
02
The Fed's next move is now a hike, not a cut. Markets have fully repriced. The 10Y yield at 4.59% and the 30Y at 5.12% are their highest levels in roughly a year. Bond markets are front-running a rate hike cycle at a time when US public debt stands at 100% of GDP. The cost of servicing that debt at these yields is a slow-moving but serious fiscal risk that equities have not yet fully priced.
03
The AI valuation story is splitting into two tracks. Enterprise AI (Anthropic at $900B, Claude Code at $44B revenue run rate) is accelerating with real margins and real revenue. But public AI equities (Nvidia, AMD, Micron) are correcting after an unsustainable near-vertical move. The private market is saying "this is real"; the public market is saying "we went too far too fast." Both can be true simultaneously.
